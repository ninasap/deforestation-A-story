04
The Causes

What's Driving the Destruction

The pressures are almost entirely human-made. Agriculture — particularly beef and soy — accounts for the majority of forest clearing, followed by logging and infrastructure expansion.

Agriculture — 73%
Cattle ranching, soy cultivation, and palm oil plantations are the leading drivers worldwide.
Logging — 10%
Both legal and illegal timber extraction degrades forest structure even without full clearing.
Wildfires — 8%
Many fires are deliberately set to clear land; climate change makes natural ignition more likely.
Infrastructure — 9%
Roads, dams, and mining fragment ecosystems and open access for further encroachment.
05
The Consequences

What We Lose When Trees Fall

Forests are not just trees. They are the habitat of 80% of land-based species, the lungs of the climate system, and the water towers of entire continents. Their loss is irreversible on human timescales.

What Comes Next
